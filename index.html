Top/front/side views, isometric drawings, rotations of 3-D objects, transformations on the coordinate plane.

Unit 1 — Square roots & Pythagoras
Square root
If n = k², then √n = k (k ≥ 0)
Pythagorean theorem
a² + b² = c²
a, b are legs; c is the hypotenuse (longest side, opposite the right angle).
Finding the hypotenuse
c = √(a² + b²)
Finding a leg
a = √(c² − b²)
Common Pythagorean triples
3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, 7-24-25
Multiples also work: 6-8-10, 9-12-15, etc.
Converse of Pythagoras
If a² + b² = c², the triangle is a right triangle
Unit 2 — Integers
Sign rules — × and ÷
(+)(+) = +  (−)(−) = +  (+)(−) = −  (−)(+) = −
Order of operations
B E DM AS
Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication (left to right), Addition/Subtraction (left to right).
Subtracting an integer
a − b = a + (−b)
Unit 3 — Fractions
Multiplying fractions
a/b × c/d = (a × c) / (b × d)
Dividing fractions
a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c
Keep the first, change ÷ to ×, flip the second.
Mixed → improper
a b/c = (a × c + b) / c
Reciprocal
Reciprocal of a/b is b/a
Unit 4 — Prisms & cylinders
Surface area — rectangular prism
SA = 2(ℓw + ℓh + wh)
Surface area — triangular prism
SA = 2(½bh) + (perimeter × length)
Surface area — cylinder
SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
Volume — any prism
V = (area of base) × height
Volume — cylinder
V = πr² × h
Circumference
C = 2πr = πd
Area of a circle
A = πr²
Unit 5 — Percent, ratio, rate
Percent of a number
p% of n = (p ÷ 100) × n
Percent change
% change = (change ÷ original) × 100
Always divide by the ORIGINAL, never the new value.
Add tax of t%
× (1 + t/100)
Apply discount of d%
× (1 − d/100)
Solving a proportion
a/b = c/d → a × d = b × c
Unit rate
first quantity ÷ second quantity
m/s ↔ km/h
m/s × 3.6 = km/h
Unit 6 — Linear equations & graphing
Preservation of equality
Whatever you do to one side, do to the other.
Distributive property
a(x + b) = ax + ab
Distributing a negative
−(x + b) = −x − b
General linear relation
y = ax + b
Ordered pair
(x, y) x = independent, y = dependent
Unit 7 — Data & probability
Probability of an event
P(E) = (favourable) ÷ (total)
Two independent events
P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
Three or more independent events
P(A and B and C) = P(A) × P(B) × P(C)
Total outcomes
(outcomes A) × (outcomes B) × …
Complement
P(not E) = 1 − P(E)
Unit 8 — Geometry
Translation notation
[a, b] → a right (− = left), b up (− = down)
Reflection in x-axis
(x, y) → (x, −y)
Reflection in y-axis
(x, y) → (−x, y)
90° rotation about origin (CW)
(x, y) → (y, −x)
90° rotation about origin (CCW)
(x, y) → (−y, x)
180° rotation about origin
(x, y) → (−x, −y)

Test-day strategy

Read every word. "What fraction is left" is different from "what fraction was eaten." Underline what's actually asked.

Show every step. Even on multiple choice, write the steps so you can catch your own mistakes.

Check your units. cm vs. m, cm² vs. cm³, % vs. decimal — wrong unit = wrong answer.

Estimate first. If a soup-can volume comes out to 50 000 cm³, you've made an error.

Verify the answer. Plug your equation answer back in. Probabilities must be between 0 and 1.

Don't get stuck. If a question takes more than 3 minutes, mark it and move on. Come back later.

Common pitfalls — units 1 to 4

√36 ≠ 18. The square root of 36 is 6, not half of 36.
Pythagoras direction. Adding squares gives c². Subtracting gives a leg squared.
Negative × negative is positive. (−4) × (−3) = +12.
(−6)² = 36 but −6² = −36. Brackets matter.
Multiplying fractions ≠ adding them. 2/3 × 1/4 = 1/6. No common denominator needed.
Mixed numbers in × or ÷: always convert to improper fractions first.
Diameter vs. radius. If given d, divide by 2 to get r.
Cylinder net has two circles AND a rectangle of length 2πr.

Common pitfalls — unit 5

0.65% = 0.0065, not 0.65.
Percent change uses the ORIGINAL. Never divide by the new value.
Successive percents don't add. 20% then 10% off = 28% off, not 30% (0.80 × 0.90 = 0.72).
Part-to-part vs. part-to-whole. 5 golf : 8 tennis is 5:8, NOT 5/13 of the balls.
When scaling a ratio, multiply ALL terms by the same number.

Common pitfalls — unit 6

Do the same thing to BOTH sides. Solving 2x + 3 = 11 means subtract 3 from both sides.
Distribute to every term. 4(x − 3) = 4x − 12, not 4x − 3.
Distributing a negative. −(x − 5) = −x + 5, not −x − 5.
If −x = 5, then x = −5. Multiply both sides by −1.
Verify by substitution. Plug your solution into the original equation.
Discrete data points are NOT joined. Number of guests, number of pizzas — leave them unconnected.

Common pitfalls — unit 7

Match graph type to data. Line for time, circle for parts of a whole, bar for categories.
Watch the y-axis scale. A bar starting at 98 makes a small difference look huge.
Independent vs. dependent. Multiplication rule only works for independent events.
"AND" means multiply. P(red AND a 6) = P(red) × P(6).
Probability is between 0 and 1. 7/4 means you've made an error.

Common pitfalls — unit 8

Top view = length × width (looking down). Front and side views show height.
Internal lines only where depth changes.
Hidden cubes can be there. One set of views can match more than one object.
Rotation direction matters. 90° CW and CCW give different images.
Reflection axis matters. x-axis flips y; y-axis flips x.
Order of transformations matters.
